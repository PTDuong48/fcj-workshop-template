> Below are my personal reflections and honest feedback about my experience in the **First Cloud Journey (FCJ) program**, with the hope of contributing to its continuous improvement.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Learning & Working Environment**
The FCJ environment is very dynamic and friendly. The FCAJ community regularly organizes insightful events such as **FCAJ Community Day** — where speakers from major companies share real-world experiences on AWS, AI Agents, and the latest technology trends. These sessions helped me not only expand my knowledge but also build a professional network naturally and effectively.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**
The Mentors were always available to guide and provide clear feedback, especially during the architecture design phase for the **DocuFlow AI** project. The "guided questioning" mentorship style, rather than simply providing answers, helped me sharpen my analytical thinking and technical decision-making. The Admin Team was also thorough in administrative support, allowing teams to stay focused on the technical work.

**3. Relevance of Content to Real-World Practice**
The learning roadmap — from foundational services (EC2, S3, VPC) to advanced topics (Serverless, Step Functions, Bedrock, Textract) — is very systematic and well-connected. The 60+ hands-on Labs spread across 7 weeks helped me build a solid foundation before entering the real project phase.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**
I gained numerous practical skills: designing Serverless architectures, writing SAM templates (IaC), configuring IAM and security, setting up monitoring with CloudWatch, and working in a team using a service ownership model. These are experiences I simply cannot get from textbooks or personal projects alone.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit**
The team spirit within our group was excellent. Even though each member was responsible for a separate part (Frontend, OCR/AI, Data, Security...), we consistently synced up, supported each other when encountering bugs, and successfully ran the final demo together. This sense of solidarity is what I valued and appreciated most.

**6. Program Policies & Support for Learners**
The program provides rich learning materials, a clear Lab system, and regularly organizes community networking events. This is a significant advantage compared to self-studying on standard online platforms.

---

### What I Was Most Satisfied With

The thing I was most satisfied with was **directly building a complete, real-world cloud system from scratch** — from ideation, architecture design, coding, and security configuration, all the way to running a live demo in front of the Mentors. That experience gave me a deep understanding of why each AWS service was chosen and how they work together in a complex system.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

* **Integrate hands-on practice earlier:** It would be beneficial to incorporate small mini-projects during the foundational learning phase (Weeks 1–7) so learners can immediately apply what they learn, rather than only doing isolated labs.
* **More real-world enterprise sharing:** It would be great to invite more speakers from companies actively using AWS to share their real production architectures and lessons learned — not just at community events, but integrated directly into the curriculum.
* **Personally:** I am very eager to continue participating in advanced programs or the AWS community after this internship concludes.
